## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of Veridian Freight & Logistics information systems, networks, devices, and data. Veridian operates in a hybrid work environment across its headquarters and two regional offices, relies on AWS cloud infrastructure and Microsoft 365 for core business operations, and stores customer personally identifiable information (PII) and processes payment card data through a third-party processor. As the company grows and enterprise clients increasingly require security attestations, formalized rules around how employees interact with company systems are essential. This policy exists to protect Veridian's systems and data, its customers, and its employees from risks that arise from improper or unauthorized use.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All Veridian Freight & Logistics employees, contractors, temporary staff, and third-party vendors with access to Veridian systems
- All company-owned and personally-owned devices used to access Veridian systems, networks, or data
- All Veridian information systems, including but not limited to: AWS cloud infrastructure, Microsoft 365 (email, Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive), the Veridian logistics tracking web application, SaaS platforms used for HR, payroll, and CRM, and any network or system accessed remotely via VPN
- All locations including headquarters, regional offices, and remote work environments

---

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 General Use and Ownership

3.1.1 All Veridian information systems, devices, networks, and data are the property of Veridian Freight & Logistics regardless of where they are located or how they are accessed.

3.1.2 Employees must have no expectation of privacy when using Veridian systems. Veridian reserves the right to monitor, access, review, and audit all activity conducted on its systems at any time and without prior notice.

3.1.3 Company systems are provided for business purposes. Limited, incidental personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with job performance, consume excessive resources, violate this or any other Veridian policy, or create legal or security risk for the company.

3.1.4 Employees are responsible for any activity conducted under their Veridian credentials. Credentials may not be shared under any circumstances.

### 3.2 Acceptable Use

3.2.1 Employees may use Veridian systems including AWS-hosted services, Microsoft 365, and the Veridian logistics tracking application for work-related tasks including communication, document creation and storage, data analysis, customer service, and logistics operations.

3.2.2 Employees may use Microsoft 365 tools (Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive) for collaboration, scheduling, and file sharing in support of business operations.

3.2.3 Employees may access the internet through Veridian-provided or approved connections for purposes directly related to their job responsibilities.

3.2.4 Limited personal use of company-provided internet access is permitted during non-work hours provided it complies with all sections of this policy.

### 3.3 Prohibited Use

The following activities are prohibited on all Veridian systems, networks, and devices:

3.3.1 Sharing login credentials, passwords, or access tokens with any other person, including other Veridian employees.

3.3.2 Installing, downloading, or executing software, applications, browser extensions, or scripts on Veridian systems or AWS-hosted infrastructure without prior written approval from IT.

3.3.3 Accessing, copying, modifying, or transmitting data that is beyond the scope of an employee's assigned job responsibilities.

3.3.4 Circumventing, disabling, or attempting to bypass any security control including multi-factor authentication, endpoint protection, network monitoring tools, or access restrictions on the Veridian logistics tracking application or any other system.

3.3.5 Using Veridian systems for any activity that is illegal, fraudulent, threatening, harassing, or in violation of applicable law.

3.3.6 Storing, transmitting, or displaying content that is discriminatory, obscene, or otherwise inappropriate in a professional environment.

3.3.7 Using Veridian systems or credentials to access, interfere with, or probe systems owned by other organizations without explicit authorization.

3.3.8 Connecting unauthorized devices to the Veridian network or AWS environment.

3.3.9 Using company systems to mine cryptocurrency, run personal servers, or conduct personal business activities.

### 3.4 Email and Internet Use

3.4.1 Veridian Microsoft 365 email accounts are for business communication. Employees must not configure automatic forwarding of company email to personal email accounts (Gmail, Yahoo, or similar).

3.4.2 Employees must not transmit customer PII, payment card data, or other sensitive Veridian data through personal email, personal messaging applications, or any non-Veridian channel.

3.4.3 Employees must exercise caution with all incoming email. Employees must not click links or open attachments in unsolicited or suspicious email and must report suspected phishing to IT immediately using the designated reporting process.

3.4.4 Employees must not use company email to subscribe to personal mailing lists, newsletters, or services unrelated to their job function.

3.4.5 Internet access through Veridian systems may not be used to access illegal content, gambling sites, or content that violates this policy.

### 3.5 Personal and Mobile Devices (BYOD)

3.5.1 Employees who work remotely or in a hybrid capacity may use personally-owned devices to access Veridian systems only when a company-issued device is unavailable and only with prior approval from their manager and IT.

3.5.2 Personally-owned devices used to access Veridian systems must meet the following minimum requirements: current operating system with security updates applied, active endpoint protection software, and screen lock enabled with a PIN or biometric.

3.5.3 Employees must enroll personally-owned devices in Veridian's mobile device management (MDM) program before accessing Microsoft 365 email, SharePoint, or OneDrive on a personal device.

3.5.4 Veridian reserves the right to remotely wipe company data from any enrolled personal device if the device is lost, stolen, or the employee separates from the company.

3.5.5 Veridian does not accept liability for personally-owned devices and will not reimburse employees for personal device costs.

3.5.6 Employees must not store Veridian customer PII or payment card data on personally-owned devices under any circumstances.

### 3.6 Data Handling

3.6.1 Customer PII collected and stored by Veridian, including names, addresses, and contact information, must be accessed only by employees whose job function requires it and must be handled in accordance with Veridian's data classification and retention standards.

3.6.2 Payment card data processed through Veridian's third-party payment processor must never be stored, copied, or transmitted outside of the approved processing channel. Employees must not record, screenshot, or retain any payment card information.

3.6.3 Sensitive data must not be transmitted via unencrypted channels. Employees must use Microsoft 365 approved tools for sharing documents containing sensitive data and must not use USB drives, personal cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud), or personal email for this purpose.

3.6.4 Employees who handle customer data as part of their role must complete data handling training as assigned by the Security Analyst.

### 3.7 Monitoring

3.7.1 Veridian Freight & Logistics monitors activity on its systems, networks, and devices. This includes but is not limited to: email content and metadata, internet activity, file access and transfer activity, authentication events, and activity within AWS infrastructure and the Veridian logistics tracking application.

3.7.2 Monitoring is conducted for security, operational, and compliance purposes. Employees have no expectation of privacy when using Veridian systems.

3.7.3 Monitoring data may be used in disciplinary proceedings or provided to law enforcement if required.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| All Employees | Read, acknowledge, and comply with this policy. Report suspected violations or security incidents to IT immediately. |
| Managers | Ensure direct reports have read and acknowledged this policy. Report suspected violations to IT and HR. |
| IT Department | Enforce technical controls that support this policy. Review and approve software installation requests. Manage device enrollment and monitoring. |
| Security Analyst | Own and maintain this policy. Conduct annual review. Investigate violations in coordination with IT and HR. |
| HR Department | Incorporate policy acknowledgment into onboarding. Coordinate disciplinary action for violations in accordance with company policy. |

---

## 5. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment. Violations that constitute criminal activity will be reported to appropriate law enforcement authorities. Veridian reserves the right to revoke system access immediately upon discovery of a suspected violation pending investigation.

---

## 6. Review Cadence

This policy will be reviewed annually by the Security Analyst and updated as needed to reflect changes in Veridian's environment, operations, or applicable law. The policy will also be reviewed following any significant security incident that reveals a gap in current policy coverage.
